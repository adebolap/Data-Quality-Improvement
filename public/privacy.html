Privacy Policy

Last updated: 28 June 2026  |  Effective date: 1 January 2026

Simply HR is committed to GDPR compliance. This policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, how long we keep it, and what rights you and your employees have.

1. Who we are

Simply HR ("we", "our", "the platform") is a human resources management platform designed for small and medium-sized businesses across Europe and Africa. References to "you" mean the organisation that has signed up to use the platform. References to "employees" mean the individuals whose data you manage using the platform.

For GDPR purposes: you are the Data Controller for your employees' personal data. We are the Data Processor acting on your instructions.

2. Data we process on your behalf

When you use Simply HR to manage your workforce, we process the following categories of personal data:

  • Identity data: first name, last name, date of birth
  • Contact data: email address, phone number, home address, city, country
  • Employment data: job title, department, hire date, salary, currency, employment status
  • Tax and compliance data: tax ID / national ID (optional), work permit details and expiry
  • Leave data: leave requests, dates, types, approval status
  • Recruitment data: CV content, application status, AI screening scores (where used)
  • Emergency contact data: name, relationship, phone number
  • Consent records: GDPR consent date and withdrawal records

3. Legal bases for processing

We process personal data under the following lawful bases:

  • Contract performance: processing employment and payroll-related data to manage your workforce obligations
  • Legal obligation: data required for tax filings, work permit records, statutory leave compliance
  • Legitimate interests: platform analytics, security monitoring, product improvement (aggregated, anonymised)
  • Consent: marketing communications to platform administrators (you may withdraw at any time)

4. AI-assisted features and automated processing

Simply HR includes AI-powered features including CV screening and candidate scoring. Under the EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689), CV screening that influences employment decisions is classified as a high-risk AI system.

Our commitments:

  • AI screening scores are advisory only — no hiring decision is made automatically without human review
  • Candidates are informed when AI is used to evaluate their application
  • Scores are explainable: you can see which criteria drove each score
  • You can override or disregard any AI score at any time
  • CV content sent to our AI provider (Groq / Google Gemini) is processed under strict data processing agreements and is not used to train third-party models

See our Security & AI Statement for full technical detail.

5. Data retention

We follow data minimisation and purpose limitation principles. Default retention periods:

  • Active employee records: retained while the employment relationship is active
  • Terminated employees: 7 years after termination (statutory minimum for tax/payroll records in most jurisdictions)
  • Unsuccessful candidates: 6 months after rejection, unless longer retention is legally required
  • Leave records: 5 years
  • Audit logs: 2 years

Per-employee retention overrides are available directly in the platform (Employee Profile → Data Retention).

6. Data transfers

Your data is processed within the EU/EEA by default. Where we use sub-processors outside the EEA (e.g., AI inference providers), we ensure:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) are in place
  • Transfer Impact Assessments are conducted
  • Data minimisation: only the content required for the task is sent

7. Sub-processors

We use the following categories of sub-processors to deliver the platform:

  • Cloud infrastructure: Vercel (hosting), MongoDB Atlas (database)
  • AI inference: Groq, Google Gemini (CV screening only, under DPA)
  • Email delivery: where email notifications are enabled

8. Your employees' rights (GDPR Articles 15-22)

Individuals whose data you manage have the following rights, which you as Data Controller are responsible for fulfilling. Simply HR provides tools to help you respond:

  • Right of access (Art. 15): export employee data from the platform in one click
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16): edit any record directly
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17): delete employee record (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Right to restrict processing (Art. 18): mark a record as restricted pending dispute
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20): export in JSON or CSV format
  • Right to object (Art. 21): flag and disable AI scoring for specific individuals

9. Security

We apply technical and organisational measures to protect personal data including encryption in transit (TLS 1.3), encryption at rest, access controls, and regular security reviews. See our Security Statement for details.

10. Cookies

The platform uses session cookies required for authentication and security. We do not use advertising or tracking cookies. Analytics, where enabled, is privacy-preserving and aggregated.

11. Contact

For privacy questions, data subject access requests, or to exercise your rights, contact us at: privacy@simplyhr.io

If you are located in the EU and believe we have not addressed your concern, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your national supervisory authority (e.g., the Belgian DPA at gegevensbeschermingsautoriteit.be).
